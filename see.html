Culture and Tradition

Tokyo is modern city that is steeped in tradition and culture. The embrace of modern culture is done in such a way that tradition and old Tokyo will never be out of site or left behind. These are a few of my favorite temples and shrines around Tokyo. They may or may not be listed in order by my favorites. They're all great. if not, they wouldn't be on the list!

  • Meiji-jingu Shrine in Harajuku
  • Tenno-ji Temple in Yanaka overlooks Tenno-ji Cemetary. It's just around the corner from Nippori Station.
  • Hie Jinja Shrine is nestled between government buildings and the business district of Asakasa on a hilltop
  • Asakusa-jinja Shrine in Asakusa. It's near the very popular Senso-ji Temple, so you may miss it.
  • Senso-ji Temple in Asakusa is considered the spiritual heart of Tokyo.

Perfect Day in Tokyo

Get up early, make sure you're wearing comfortable shoes and enjoy!

  • Start at Harajuki Station
  • Walk to the entrance of Meiju Shrine. Spend time in the tree filled oasis in the middle of the city.
  • Leave the shrine via the same gate and head to Takeshita Dori Street, across the street from Harajuku Station.
  • Join the throngs of people along this narrow street and check out all of the shops.
  • From Harajuku Station, take the walk towards Yoyogi national Stadium to get to Shibuya. The center of youth culture and fashion in Tokyo.
  • To end the day, take the train to Shinjuku Station to have a great dinner, find a bar and have an Asahi or Sapporro and take in the sights
  • Head back to your hotel room and rest up for tomorrow. Your feet will thank you.
